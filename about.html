The Founder

His journey into breathwork started on the starting line of his first triathlon, where he discovered that the ability to control his breath was the single most powerful tool for managing stress, focus, and performance under pressure.

The Practice

Why breathwork works

Breathwork is one of the most evidence-based methods for regulating your autonomic nervous system. Unlike meditation, which works primarily through attention, breathwork acts directly on the vagus nerve through mechanical and chemical pathways. You can change your heart rate, cortisol levels, and blood pressure within minutes, simply by changing how you breathe.

Your autonomic nervous system has two branches. The sympathetic branch drives the "fight or flight" response: elevated heart rate, shallow breathing, heightened alertness. The parasympathetic branch is "rest and digest": calm, recovery, and restoration. Most people are stuck in sympathetic dominance because of constant stress, screen time, and overstimulation. Breathwork gives you a direct switch between these states.

Vagus Nerve Activation

Extended exhales stimulate the vagus nerve, triggering parasympathetic responses. Reduced heart rate, lower blood pressure, and a measurable sense of calm.

CO2 Tolerance

Breath holds and controlled breathing improve your body's tolerance to CO2. This reduces anxiety sensitivity and builds lasting stress resilience.

Heart Rate Variability

Techniques like Coherent Breathing at 5.5 breaths per minute maximize HRV. This is the gold standard biomarker for cardiovascular health and emotional regulation.

Cortisol Reduction

Regular breathwork practice has been shown to reduce cortisol levels by 20 to 30 percent. Less cortisol means better sleep, clearer thinking, and more energy.
