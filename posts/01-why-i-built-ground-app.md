# Why I Built Ground App: From Breathwork Instructor to App Creator

*By Collin Chartier, Founder of Ground App*

---

I never planned to build an app. I'm a breathwork instructor. I coach people through breathing — in person, one-on-one, in groups. I watch their shoulders drop, their jaw unclench, their eyes soften after three minutes of controlled breathing. That moment when someone shifts from sympathetic overdrive into parasympathetic calm is the most rewarding thing I do.

But I kept running into the same problem.

## The Gap Between Sessions

My clients would leave a session feeling incredible — regulated, grounded, present in their body. Then they'd go back to their inbox, their commute, their 14-hour workday. By the time our next session came around, they were right back where they started: chest tight, breathing shallow, nervous system on fire.

They'd try breathwork apps. Generic ones with countdown timers and ambient sounds. But none of them answered the question my clients actually needed answered: **"I feel anxious right now — what should I breathe?"**

That's the question that started Ground App.

## The Problem with Every Breathwork App I Tried

I downloaded every breathwork app on the App Store. Calm, Breathwrk, Oak, Othership, Wim Hof — I tried them all. They all did the same thing: gave you a menu of techniques and let you pick one.

But here's the thing about someone in sympathetic overdrive: **they can't choose well.** When you're anxious, your prefrontal cortex — the part of your brain that makes rational decisions — is suppressed. Asking someone in a stress response to browse a menu of 30 breathing techniques and pick the right one is like asking someone drowning to choose which life raft to swim to.

What they need is for someone to say: *"I hear you. Here's exactly what to do right now. Follow my voice."*

That's what I do in person. That's what I wanted an app to do.

## Building the App I Wished Existed

Ground App works the way a good breathwork session works. You open the app and tell it how you feel — in your own words. "I'm anxious before a presentation." "I just had a fight with my partner and I can't calm down." "I need energy for my 5am workout."

The AI listens. It selects the right technique — calming (4-7-8 Breathing, Physiological Sigh), energizing (Wim Hof, Breath of Fire), or balancing (Coherent Breathing, Box Breathing). It sets the timing for your experience level. And it guides you through it with voice narration.

No menus. No decision fatigue. Just: tell me what's happening, and breathe.

## The Voice Cloning Breakthrough

The feature I'm most proud of is voice cloning. Here's why.

In my coaching, I noticed something: clients responded differently depending on whose voice guided them. Some connected with mine. But the deepest sessions — the ones where someone genuinely dropped into their body — happened when the guidance felt like it was coming from *within* them, not from an external source.

So I asked: what if you could guide yourself in your own voice?

Ground App uses ElevenLabs AI voice technology. You record 30 seconds of your voice — just talking naturally — and the app creates a high-fidelity clone. From then on, every breathing session is narrated by you. "Inhale... hold... exhale..." in your own voice.

It sounds simple. But when you close your eyes and hear your own voice gently guiding you through a breath cycle, something shifts. It's not an app anymore. It's you, talking to yourself, bringing yourself back to ground.

That's the experience I wanted to build.

## What "Ground" Means

The name isn't an accident. From the mind into the body — this is where we feel grounded.

Every breathwork technique in the app is a tool for the same thing: getting out of your head and into your body. When your nervous system is dysregulated — when you're anxious, scattered, burned out, overstimulated — you're living in your mind. Ruminating, catastrophizing, replaying. Breathwork is the bridge back to your body.

Ground App is that bridge in your pocket.

## Who This Is For

I built Ground App for the people I coach:

- **The athlete** who needs to dial in their nervous system before competition
- **The new parent** who lies awake at 2am with a racing mind

If you've ever needed someone to tell you exactly how to breathe in a moment of stress — and wished that someone could be you — Ground App is what I built.
